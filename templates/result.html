🔎 What do these methods mean?

Rule-based Detection: This method uses predefined rules written by experts. For example, it flags emails with suspicious keywords like “urgent” or “account locked,” checks sender addresses, and looks for mismatched links. It’s fast and transparent, but can miss new tricks.

ML-based Detection: This method uses a machine learning model trained on large datasets of phishing and safe emails. It learns patterns automatically and adapts to new styles of phishing. It provides a confidence score, but is less transparent than rules.

Together, these two approaches give a layered defense: rules catch obvious threats quickly, while ML adapts to evolving phishing tactics.
